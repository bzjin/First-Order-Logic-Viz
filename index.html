Tableaux Visualized

Beatrice Jin CS4860 Fall 2016 Final Project

A tableaux proof for a formula X is constructed by iteratively extending the root tableau FX until it is complete. If the resulting tableau is closed, the formula is true, otherwise it is not. A tableau can be extended by applying either an α-rule or a β-rule.

The α-rules decompose as follows:

  • T(X∧Y)
    TX
    TY
  • F(X∨Y)
    FX
    FY
  • F(X⇒Y)
    TX
    FY
  • T(¬X)
    FX
  • F(¬X)
    TX

And the β-rules decompose as follows (β-formulas are underlined throughout the visualization):

  • F(X∧Y)
    FX
    FY
  • T(X∨Y)
    TX
    TY
  • T(X⇒Y)
    FX
    TY

We see that every rule decomposes the formula, so the process must eventually terminate.

The original Tableaux method

The simplest method of decomposing a formula is by top-down order and mark them off as used.

Block Tableaux

The original tableaux method is makes it difficult for humans to keep track of the formulas that have and haven't been decomposed. One way of redesigning tableaux decomposition is to support local reasoning, where each formula is still a node, but each β-formula decomposes into two nodes, side by side.
